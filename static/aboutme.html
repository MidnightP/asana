About Me

Born and raised in a small town in Northeast Pennsylvania, USA, I developed a sense of adventure and strong curiosity from a young age. After completing my B.S. at Temple University in Philadelphia (and discovering yoga along the way), I took a leap of courage and moved to a small village in Thailand to teach English and science. I backpacked Asia on my own and created lasting, vivid memories while viewing the world through my camera lens. After relocating to the Netherlands for my M.S. at Utrecht University and settling (for now) in Nijmegen, where I've since become an Ashtanga yoga instructor, my current path is dedicated to learning everything I can from web development and working towards a career path as a developer and yoga instructor.

Things I like: Ashtanga yoga, rock climbing (which I managed to discover while living in one of the flattest countries in the world), vegetarianism and nutrition, art and architecture ranging from Art Nouveau, Art Deco, and Modernism, and the eternal quest to better myself.

I strive to live my life by the words of Sri Patthabi Jois, "Practice, and all is coming."
